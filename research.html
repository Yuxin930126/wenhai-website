Pore-scale multiphase flow and interfacial physics in porous media

I study how wettability, pore geometry, and particle-mediated interfaces regulate multiphase flow configurations and transport dynamics.

1. Wettability and Interface Morphology

How surface wettability regulates interface morphology during multiphase displacement. I examine transition conditions between capillary-controlled and viscous-controlled regimes.

2. Geometry-Induced Flow Pattern Transitions

How pore geometry, depth variation, and connectivity alter fluid configurations and trapping. I identify geometry-triggered displacement transitions in depth-variable and heterogeneous porous networks.

3. Particle-Mediated Interfacial Transport

How micro/nanoparticle suspensions interact with interfaces during displacement. I study particle-driven reversal of capillary trapping and instability control.
